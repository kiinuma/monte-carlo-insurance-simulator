Monte Carlo Insurance Loss Simulator

Interactive actuarial modeling — compound Binomial–Uniform model

Overview

This tool simulates the aggregate annual losses of an auto insurance portfolio using a compound probability model. Set portfolio parameters — drivers, claim probability, severity range — and the simulator runs thousands of independent one-year scenarios to estimate the distribution of total losses.

The key output is the solvency reserve: the amount an insurer must hold to cover losses with 95% confidence. Two methods are compared: a closed-form CLT estimate and an empirical 95th percentile drawn directly from the simulated distribution.

The Loss Triangle tab implements the chain-ladder method — the standard actuarial technique for estimating IBNR reserves from historical loss development patterns.

The Model

  • Claim frequency — each driver independently files a claim with probability p, so claim count follows Binomial(N, p).
  • Claim severity — each claim amount is drawn from Uniform(min, max).
  • Aggregate loss — total portfolio loss S is a compound random variable; moments derived via Law of Total Expectation and Variance.
  • CLT reserve — for large portfolios CLT approximates S as Normal, giving E[S] + 1.645·Std[S].

Parameters

  • Drivers (N) — total policyholders. Larger portfolios produce tighter, more bell-shaped distributions.
  • P(claim) — annual claim probability per driver. At 5%, ~500 of 10,000 drivers file claims.
  • Min / Max Severity — bounds of the Uniform claim cost distribution.
  • Premium Loading — markup above expected losses to cover expenses and profit.

▸ How It Works — Compound Binomial–Uniform Model click to expand

Frequency

N ~ Binomial(n, p)
E[N] = n · p
Var[N] = n · p · (1−p)

Severity

X ~ Uniform(a, b)
E[X] = (a + b) / 2
Var[X] = (b − a)² / 12

Aggregate (CLT)

E[S] = E[N] · E[X]
Var[S] = E[N]·Var[X] + Var[N]·E[X]²
Reserve = E[S] + 1.645 · Std[S]

Loss Development Triangle

Rows = accident years. Columns = development periods.

Cell [i, j] = cumulative paid losses for accident year i at development period j.

Upper-left = observed data. Lower-right = unknown IBNR.

Development Factors

f_j = Σ C[i, j+1] / Σ C[i, j]

Summed over rows where both periods are known.

Weighted average captures historical loss development patterns.

IBNR Projection

C[i, j] = C[i, j−1] × f_{j−1}

IBNR_i = C[i, ult] − C[i, latest]

Total IBNR = reserve the insurer must hold for unreported losses.
